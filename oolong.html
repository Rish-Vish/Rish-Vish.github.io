Oolong Tea

Background image: Oolong tea leaves rolled by Simon A. Eugster, CC BY-SA 4.0.

Oolong tea falls between green and black tea in terms of oxidation. It offers a wide range of flavors, depending on how it's processed.

Origins and Varieties

Oolong tea is primarily produced in China and Taiwan. The level of oxidation can range from 8% to 85%, resulting in a diverse range of flavors and aromas. Popular varieties include Tieguanyin (Iron Goddess of Mercy) and Dong Ding.

Flavor Profiles

Oolong tea can range from floral and fruity to roasted and nutty. Some oolongs have a light, delicate flavor, while others are rich and complex.

Preparation

Oolong tea is typically brewed with water between 180-200°F (82-93°C) for 3-7 minutes, depending on the variety. Multiple infusions are common, with each infusion yielding a slightly different flavor profile.
