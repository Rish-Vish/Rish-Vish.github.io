Green Tea

Background image: Green tea leaves by Fir0002, CC BY-SA 3.0.

Green tea is made from unoxidized leaves and is one of the less processed types of tea. It is known for its high antioxidant content and refreshing, slightly grassy flavor.

Origins and Varieties

Green tea originated in China and has been consumed for thousands of years. Popular varieties include Sencha from Japan, Matcha, also from Japan, and Gunpowder from China.

Benefits

  1. Rich in antioxidants, which may reduce the risk of chronic diseases.
  2. May improve brain function and mental clarity.
  3. Can aid in weight loss and metabolism.

Preparation

Green tea is best brewed with water between 160-180°F (70-82°C) for 2-3 minutes to avoid a bitter taste. It is generally not served with milk or sugar.
